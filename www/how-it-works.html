Your Loan And Repaying It:

Your lender will be one of a number of approved responsible lenders that abide by a strict code of practice in assessing and issuing loans.

Lenders use a number of different criteria to evaluate your suitability for a loan including your income, home ownership status and financial stability amongst other things. They do this by running a credit check as well as conducting a number of other checks to verify your identity and bank details.

All lenders will expect you to pay your loan back on your due date or by installments that you have set up. You should ensure you have enough money available in your account for this repayment. If in any circumstances you do not have enough money to pay back your loan you should immediately contact your lender to work out a repayment plan and discuss the options. If you are late in making a repayment your lender may charge you a fee: this will vary per lender.

Lenders will use a number of options to collect your loan repayments from you depending on your credit status. The two most common methods used are:

  • Asking you to set up a direct debit
  • Asking you to authorise the lender to make direct collections from your nominated debit card.

This process is often referred to as auto-withdraw or continuous payment authority (CPA). You have the right to cancel a CPA by contacting your lender directly and initiating the process.
